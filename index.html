Red Flag Triage Checklist

Spot two or more of these? Stop interacting and escalate immediately.

🚨

Display Name Mismatch

Shows "CEO" but email is @gmail.com. Always check the header.

🎭

Domain Spoofing / Typos

paypa1.com, amaz0n.com, or foreign character swaps (Homoglyphs).

⏳

Extreme Urgency

"IMMEDIATE", "30 minutes", "Act now" — triggers fight-or-flight.

🤫

Secrecy / Bypass Rules

"Do not tell anyone", "Bypass standard procedure" — classic BEC.

💰

Sensitive Info Requests

Passwords, MFA codes, wire transfers, or payment changes.

📎

Dangerous Attachments

.iso, .js, .scr, .html (smuggling). Real invoices are .pdf.

📱

Unsolicited QR Codes

"Scan to secure account" — bypasses desktop URL filters (Quishing).

📞

Callback Scams (TOAD)

No link, just a phone number to call "support".

🔗

Subdomain Burying

Read URLs right-to-left. google.com.malicious.net → root is malicious.net.

Forensic Email Analysis

Analyzing the "CEO Wire Transfer" scam (Slide 16).

From: CEO - STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL <ceo.urgent@executive-update.com>
Subject: IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED: Transfer Authorization
Body:
"URGENT: Process the attached wire transfer instruction immediately. This is critical and must remain STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL. Do not discuss with anyone. Bypass standard procedure. Thank you."
🚩 Domain Mismatch
Display name says "CEO", but the actual domain is executive-update.com (unknown, unverified).
🧠 Psychological Triggers
Authority (CEO) + Urgency (IMMEDIATE) + Secrecy (Confidential). Overrides logic.
⚠️ Bypass Request
Explicitly asks to "Bypass standard procedure" — this is the defining red flag of a BEC attack.
📌 Final Verdict
MALICIOUS — Whaling / BEC. Block domain, escalate to SOC, alert finance.
